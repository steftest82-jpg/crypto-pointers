<h2 id="capacity-milestone">Capacity Milestone</h2>
<p>The Bitcoin Lightning Network has crossed the $1 billion total capacity threshold, a milestone that reflects growing confidence in Bitcoin's layer 2 scaling solution. This capacity — which represents the total value locked in payment channels across the network — has tripled over the past year.</p>
<p>The network now supports over 70,000 active channels connecting thousands of nodes worldwide. This interconnected web of payment channels enables Bitcoin to be sent instantly to virtually anyone on the network, settling in seconds rather than the 10-minute average of on-chain transactions.</p>
<p>Transaction fees on Lightning remain negligible, typically less than one cent regardless of the payment amount. This makes Bitcoin viable for micro-payments, everyday purchases, and cross-border remittances — use cases that were previously impractical due to on-chain fee requirements.</p>

<h2 id="use-cases-expanding">Use Cases Expanding</h2>
<p>Payment applications have been the primary driver of Lightning adoption. Strike, Cash App, and other consumer-facing apps have integrated Lightning payments, giving millions of users the ability to send Bitcoin instantly without needing to understand the underlying technology.</p>
<p>El Salvador's Bitcoin adoption has been heavily reliant on Lightning Network infrastructure. The government's Chivo wallet and numerous third-party alternatives use Lightning for the majority of Bitcoin transactions within the country, proving the technology's viability for national-scale deployment.</p>
<p>Content monetisation platforms are emerging as an innovative Lightning use case. Nostr, the decentralised social media protocol, integrates Lightning for micro-tipping and content payments. Podcasting 2.0 apps enable per-minute streaming payments to creators, creating new economic models for digital content.</p>

<h2 id="technical-improvements">Technical Improvements</h2>
<p>Recent protocol upgrades have significantly improved Lightning's reliability and usability. Taproot channels, splicing, and dual-funded channels have made the network more capital-efficient and reduced the friction of channel management for both node operators and end users.</p>
<p>Routing reliability has improved to over 99% success rates for payments under $1,000, up from roughly 95% a year ago. Pathfinding algorithms have become more sophisticated, finding optimal routes through the network with minimal fees and low latency.</p>
<p>Privacy enhancements including route blinding and payment decorrelation are making Lightning transactions more private than on-chain Bitcoin transactions. These improvements address a key criticism of Lightning — that payment routing could potentially be surveilled by network participants — and bring Lightning closer to the privacy standards expected for a digital cash system.</p>
