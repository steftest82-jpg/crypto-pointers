<h2 id="what-is-agglayer">What Is AggLayer</h2>
<p>Polygon has unveiled AggLayer, an ambitious protocol that aims to solve one of blockchain's most persistent problems: fragmentation. By aggregating zero-knowledge proofs from multiple participating chains, AggLayer creates the experience of a single, unified blockchain while preserving the sovereignty and customisability of individual chains.</p>
<p>Think of it as the internet's TCP/IP for blockchains. Just as the internet protocols unified disparate computer networks into a seamless global network, AggLayer unifies disparate blockchains into a seamless multi-chain experience where users do not need to think about which chain their assets are on.</p>
<p>The launch represents a fundamental pivot for Polygon from being a single Layer 2 solution for Ethereum to becoming an interoperability infrastructure provider for the entire blockchain ecosystem. This broader ambition is reflected in updated branding and a revised tokenomics model.</p>

<h2 id="technical-architecture">Technical Architecture</h2>
<p>AggLayer uses a pessimistic proof system that assumes all participating chains could be malicious, verifying their state transitions through zero-knowledge proofs before accepting cross-chain messages. This provides security guarantees equivalent to settling everything on Ethereum mainnet.</p>
<p>Shared liquidity is perhaps the most impactful feature. Assets deposited on any AggLayer-connected chain are effectively available across all connected chains without the need for traditional bridging. This eliminates bridge risk — historically one of the largest sources of losses in DeFi.</p>
<p>The architecture supports customisable execution environments, meaning chains connected to AggLayer can optimise for specific use cases — high-throughput gaming chains, privacy-focused payment chains, or compliance-friendly enterprise chains — while sharing a common liquidity and security layer.</p>

<h2 id="competitive-positioning">Competitive Positioning</h2>
<p>AggLayer positions Polygon in direct competition with Cosmos's Inter-Blockchain Communication (IBC) protocol and Polkadot's relay chain architecture. However, Polygon's approach benefits from Ethereum's security guarantees through ZK proofs, which neither Cosmos nor Polkadot can offer.</p>
<p>The strategic implications are significant. If AggLayer gains adoption among chain developers, Polygon becomes the connective tissue of the multi-chain future — collecting fees from every cross-chain interaction and becoming more valuable as more chains join the network.</p>
<p>For MATIC (soon to be POL) holders, the success of AggLayer could fundamentally re-rate the token from a simple L2 gas token to a critical piece of blockchain infrastructure. The transition from MATIC to POL token, designed to support the new multi-chain architecture, reflects this expanded ambition.</p>
