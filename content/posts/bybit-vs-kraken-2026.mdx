<p>Derivatives trading accounts for the majority of crypto trading volume globally. In 2026, perpetual futures alone represent over 75 percent of all crypto trades by dollar value. Bybit and Kraken are two of the strongest platforms in this space, but they target different types of traders.</p>

<p>Bybit is built for derivatives-first traders who want maximum leverage, advanced order types, and competitive fees. Kraken takes a more conservative approach with regulated futures products and a focus on security. This comparison will help you decide which platform matches your trading goals.</p>

<h2 id="platform-overviews">Platform Overviews</h2>

<p><a href="https://www.bybit.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Bybit</a> launched in 2018 and quickly grew into one of the top five exchanges by derivatives volume. The platform serves over 20 million users globally and processes more than $5 billion in daily derivatives trades. Bybit is headquartered in Dubai and holds licenses in several jurisdictions.</p>

<p>Kraken has operated since 2011 and added futures trading in 2020 through its Kraken Futures division. The exchange serves 13 million users and is one of the most trusted platforms among institutional traders. Kraken holds money transmitter licenses across the US and Europe.</p>

<p>Both exchanges also offer spot trading, staking, and OTC services. However, their derivatives offerings are where the real differences emerge. For a broader exchange comparison, see our <a href="/blog/best-crypto-exchanges-2026">top 10 crypto exchanges ranking</a>.</p>

<h2 id="derivatives-products">Derivatives Products Compared</h2>

<p>Bybit offers USDT perpetual contracts, USDC perpetual contracts, inverse perpetual contracts, and options. The platform lists over 300 derivatives trading pairs covering major and mid-cap tokens. New contracts are added weekly based on market demand.</p>

<p>Kraken offers perpetual futures on about 95 pairs through Kraken Futures. The selection is smaller but focuses on the most liquid assets where execution quality matters most. Kraken also provides fixed-maturity futures contracts, which Bybit does not offer.</p>

<p>For options trading, Bybit has a fully functional options desk with European-style contracts on BTC and ETH. Kraken does not currently offer options trading. If you need a wide variety of derivative products, Bybit covers more ground.</p>

<h2 id="fee-structures">Fee Structures</h2>

<p>Bybit charges 0.01 percent for makers and 0.06 percent for takers on perpetual contracts. This means makers actually receive a small rebate for adding liquidity to the order book. These fees are among the lowest in the industry for derivatives.</p>

<p>Kraken Futures charges 0.02 percent for makers and 0.05 percent for takers. The maker fee is slightly higher than Bybit, but the taker fee is marginally lower. For high-volume takers, <a href="https://www.kraken.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Kraken</a> may actually be cheaper.</p>

<p>Funding rates on perpetual contracts vary dynamically on both platforms. During trending markets, funding rates can significantly impact your holding costs on leveraged positions. Both exchanges display real-time funding rates clearly in their trading interfaces.</p>

<h2 id="leverage-risk">Leverage and Risk Management</h2>

<p>Bybit allows up to 100x leverage on BTC and ETH perpetual contracts, with lower maximums on altcoins. The platform supports both cross-margin and isolated-margin modes, giving you flexibility in how you manage risk across positions.</p>

<p>Kraken caps leverage at 50x on major pairs and lower on altcoins. This more conservative approach reduces the risk of rapid liquidation but limits potential returns on directional bets. Kraken also enforces position size limits based on your account tier.</p>

<p>Both platforms offer stop-loss, take-profit, and trailing stop orders. Bybit adds conditional orders and one-cancels-the-other (OCO) pairs that help automate complex strategies. If you value advanced risk management tools, Bybit provides more options out of the box.</p>

<h2 id="which-platform">Which Platform Suits You</h2>

<p>Choose Bybit if you are an experienced derivatives trader who wants maximum leverage, the widest contract selection, and the lowest maker fees. The platform rewards aggressive trading styles with competitive fee structures and advanced order types. Bybit also offers a copy trading feature where you can follow top-performing derivatives traders.</p>

<p>Choose Kraken if you prioritize security, regulatory compliance, and a more measured approach to leverage. US-based traders in particular should consider Kraken since Bybit faces access restrictions in several US states. Kraken's clean security record adds confidence for traders who keep significant capital on-exchange.</p>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

<h3>Is Bybit safe to use for derivatives trading?</h3>
<p>Bybit maintains strong security practices including cold storage, mandatory two-factor authentication, and regular third-party audits. The platform has not experienced any major hacks. However, it is not as heavily regulated as Kraken, so users in strict jurisdictions should verify local availability.</p>

<h3>Can US residents use Bybit for futures trading?</h3>
<p>Bybit restricts access for US residents in compliance with local regulations. US-based traders should use Kraken Futures or consider other regulated platforms. Using a VPN to access restricted exchanges violates terms of service and could result in account freezes.</p>

<h3>Which platform has better liquidation protection?</h3>
<p>Kraken uses a dual-price liquidation system that helps prevent cascade liquidations during volatile periods. Bybit offers an insurance fund exceeding $300 million to cover socialized losses. Both platforms are well-equipped, but Kraken's lower leverage limits inherently reduce liquidation risk.</p>
